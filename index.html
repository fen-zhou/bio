Bio.

Since 2012, Dr. Zhou is an Associate Professor at the LIA lab of the University of Avignon, France. He is a Senior Member of IEEE. From Sept. 2007 to Sept. 2010, he prepared the Ph.D. degree at the AtNet team of IRISA / INRIA Rennes in France. His Ph.D thesis was jointly supervised by Prof. Bernard Cousin and Prof. Miklos Molnar. Then he joined the computer science department of Telecom Bretagne as a post-doctoral fellow, where he worked on the European FP7 CNG project and French Zewall project until Sept. 2012. He has served as a Syposium Co-chair, Publicity Co-chairs, Local Organization Chair, and Session Chair in several International Conferences (IEEE ICNC’18, IEEE WCSP’14, IEEE Wimob’15, NetgCOOP’16, IEEE MovNet’17, IEEE Globecom’13 ect.). He has also served on the program committees for several international conferences such as IEEE INFOCOM, IEEE GLOBECOM, IEEE ONDM, and IEEE IWCMC. From Sept. 2016, he is awarded with the French Research Bonus (PEDR) for a period of four years.

Research Interests:

He is interested at developing mathematical models and heuristic algorithms for solving optimization problems in optical networks, content delivery networks (CDN), wireless Ad-Hoc Networks and Intelligent Transportation Systems:

  • Routing, Protection and Spectral Resource Allocations in Optical Networks

  • Overlay Optimization and Bandwidth Allocation for Video Streaming in CDNs

  • Message Dissemination and Routing Optimization in Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS)

  • Routing and Security in Wireless Ad-Hoc Networks

  • Integer Linear Program (ILP) modeling for network optimization problems
